The Big Picture in Dayton, February 2026

Several macro forces are converging that create a window of opportunity for a skilled, small contracting team in the Dayton metro area. Here's what the data shows.

Construction Labor Crisis

Ohio has lost construction workers while demand has surged. Nationally, the industry needs roughly half a million additional workers in 2026. In Ohio specifically, 85% of contractors reported difficulty filling positions, and 70% said workforce shortages are causing project delays. Carpenter job openings run at about 9,100 per year nationally. Dave's journeyman credentials put him in high demand.

Federal Energy Policy Shift

The "One Big Beautiful Bill" (signed July 2025) terminated the 25C Energy Efficient Home Improvement Tax Credit after December 31, 2025, and the 25D Residential Clean Energy Credit on the same date. This is a major shift — homeowners who were relying on tax credits for energy upgrades must now look to state rebate programs or utility incentives instead.

However, the HOMES and HEAR rebate programs (funded at $8.8 billion nationally) remain available through September 2031. Ohio has been allocated $240 million. The state submitted its application and was awaiting federal approval as of late 2025. These programs are separate from the terminated tax credits.

Ohio Weatherization Demand

Ohio's HWAP program has weatherized 304,000+ homes since 1977 and operates across all 88 counties through 35 sub-grantees. MVCAP serves Montgomery, Darke, Greene, and Preble counties with 9 crews working on nearly 600 homes annually. Programs report extensive waiting lists with applicants assigned priority points. MVCAP is actively hiring crew members.

Dayton Housing Market

Median home price is $145K (December 2025), up 16% year-over-year. Renovation costs have surged 67% since 2020. Half of Ohio homes are 40+ years old, creating sustained renovation demand. There are 12,000+ involuntary liens and 423 pre-foreclosure properties in the Dayton metro, suggesting distressed inventory for flipping.

⚠️ Key Risk: Federal Policy Uncertainty The IRA programs are subject to policy shifts under the current administration. Some HVAC contractors note the IRA Act "has been revoked by executive order and is under review." While HOMES/HEAR rebate funds already allocated to states appear secure, no new IRA tax credits are available. Business plans should not depend solely on federal programs.
